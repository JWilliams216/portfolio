Help Managing Life & Mental Health Case Study

Purpose, Audience, Scenario, & Goals

Purpose: I would like my website to help me accomplish a site where struggling parents can visit and find tools and tips to help them get some mental clarity and hopefully leave my site feeling a little less stressed.

Audience: Individuals using and viewing my site would typically be parents stressing about organizing their life and maintaining their mental health at the same time, you shouldn’t have to choose between them. My site could be used by professional peers, doctors, therapists, educators, and anyone looking for tools and tips to manage their life better.

Scenario: Parents who feel overwhelmed with working full-time, making sure all their kids’ needs are met, and being stressed about trying to fit all responsibilities into the hours of the day.

Goals: The primary goal of my site is to acquire knowledge about balancing kid(s), mental health, and a job. The secondary goal is to connect parents to helpful networks, resources, and tools to maintain an overall healthy life.

Development Process

I used Waterfall Development, there were four phases.

Phase One was ideas for my website, originally I wanted my site to be for single parents, after talking to a few two parent households I decided to make my site for all parents.

Phase Two was gathering content for my site, I needed to know how many sections and how much content I would need to display.

Phase Three was design, I started with making a demo site with filler text as a live wireframe to make sure all my content would fit correctly.

Phase Four was putting it all together and hosting my site. I used my demo site to make any large changes to make sure I didn't mess up my project too bad before implementing it.

Technologies & Framework: HTML, Bootstrap, and CSS when I exhausted Bootstraps designs.

Persona

Name: Tommy Thomas

Age: 31 years old

Role: Single dad of a five-year-old daughter, works full time at Smith and Sons construction company, and caretaker of house.

Technology: Prefers to use Apple products. Owns an iPhone XR with iOS 16, iPad 3 with iOS 9.3.6, and Beats Studio3 Wireless noise-canceling on ear headphones.

Frustrations and Needs: Tommy struggles with being on time to events and remembering everything that he needs to have with him, whether it be snacks or entertainment for his daughter on long car rides, his keys or wallet, or leaving clothes in the washer overnight and having to rewash them. Tommy’s daughter is enrolled in daycare that provides her meals for her but when it comes to his health he tends to forget that he’s human and needs to eat and often settles for fast food that leaves him feeling awful after he eats it or doesn’t keep him full long. At night, after Tommy puts his daughter to sleep, he’ll often spend hours trying to play catch up on his household work, and before he knows it he only has five hours before he has to be awake and out the door, which leaves him groggy and unrested almost every morning.

Attitudes and Behaviors: Works full-time at Smith and Sons construction company from 7am to 5pm. Tommy tries to be there for his daughter as much as he can by making sure he does her hair every morning, planning fun events whether it be a quick trip to the park or a visit to a zoo or aquarium. Tommy also spends a lot of time making sure his house is in order by keeping it clean, laundry, and grocery shopping.

Goals: Due to all the work, life, and kid responsibilities Tommy has to juggle each and every day he is looking for a way to manage it all without negating any of his responsibilities. He wants to be able to wake up each morning well rested, eat healthy meals to have sustainable energy throughout his day, and be able to spend the most time possible with his daughter.

User Story: As a single parent using this site, I am hoping to find tools and resources to help me manage my time efficiently and not feel so overwhelmed that I have to choose between my responsibilities.

Additional information Tommy may need in order to achieve his goals: In order for Tommy to achieve his goal he may need to speak with a nutritionist about which types of food would be best for his body. He could talk with his doctor about how to get a more restful night's sleep. Depending on his mental status he may consider talking to a therapist to help sort through his emotions and thoughts.

Milestone 2: Content Strategy

My site will be one page providing knowledge about balancing life and mental health, and will consist of the following sections.

Navigation Bar

The navigation bar will contain the title of my site, aligned left on large screens and will be the width of the screen on small screens. Responsive links to the different sections of my site will be displayed to the left on large screens and within a hamburger to the left on small screens.

Introduction

On the left will be an image, on the right will be an introduction to my site and its contents. There will not be any interaction in this section.

Children

In this section I will describe how to spend quality time with children with a time efficient bedtime routine, dancing and singing, meaningful activities, and books. Providing ways to be present and engaged with the time you have. It’ll have a heading, two cards next to each other on large screens, and they’ll stack on small screens, one will contain a list of the bedtime routine and the other will have links to different YouTube videos of songs and dances. Below the card, will be a five tiered collapsed accordion, a heading of “Meaningful Activities”, it will display meaningful activities such as, crafts, slime creation, sandbox, mud lies, zoo, etc., within each drop down will be instructions or links to each activity. Below will be twelve images books in a row with their titles beneath them, the images will have links attached to free online books.

Household

This section will have lists on how to organize commitments, schedule, chores, and a calendar. It’ll contain four columns on a large screen and will be stacked on small screens. Each with a heading, an image displaying an example of the set-up, and a list of how to set up each section, they’ll be color coded to help keep eyes focused.There will be links on the bottom of each list to additional resources such as Google Calendar, a chore chart, Google Keep, etc..

Mental Health & Self-Care

This section will have an interactive carousel displaying tips on bettering self-care and mental health by displaying slides about emotions and triggers, nutrition, sleep, breaks and relaxing, boundaries, and exercise. There will be six slides containing an image as the background, a heading and text box in front of each picture, clickable arrows on the left and right to navigate to the next and previous slides, at the sixth slide clicking the next arrow will return to the first slide. There will also be clickable lines on the bottom of the carousel to each slide.

Support & Community

In this section I explain who could be considered as a part of your community or support network and provide links to apps, groups, tiplines, and websites to places that could help expand it. The section contains two text boxes and two flush accordions with three clickable tiers below each text. Both sets of text will be strictly informative and have no interaction, on one flush accordion will be headings for groups, tiplines, apps, and websites and four cards within each tier containing images on top and a description about the site below the image and have a link to the site. On the other accordion will be headings for types of nutritionists, questions to ask doctors, and ways to communicate with therapists and contains four cards within each tier. The cards will contain images on top and description of different types of nutritionists and how each could help, a list of questions for doctors, and a list of ways to communicate with a therapist.

Footer

Text "Site designed and built by Jasmine Williams" and clickable icons with links to my GitHub and LinkedIn and professional email in the center of a black footer below text.

Design

For the design of my site I will have a color scheme that will display each section clearly and make the content easy to read, making sure that the patterns don’t distract from the content. I will be sure to review a psychology color wheel when deciding on the colors.
